Overview

The international response to the Syrian Civil War has been polarized. Some media outlets tend towards a pro-regime bias, while others tend towards an anti-regime bias. Of interest is the difference in lexica between pro-regime media and anti-regime media. The task is to create a means of classifying an article on the Syrian Civil War as either pro-regime or anti-regime based on the lexicon used in the article. This entails creating a system that categorizes articles as pro-regime or anti-regime, and attempts to determine the respective lexica.

Motivation

The task is important as it can help determine the biases inherent in media coverage of the Syrian civil war by both pro- and anti-regime news sources. It is hoped that this can help determine whether there are specific sets of vocabulary used by pro- and anti-regime media. The successful creation of a classifier would allow a more detailed view of how widespread support for the regime or opposition is. Being able to answer questions such as "Does South African media support the Syrian government or opposition?" would allow mapping of international opinion about the Syrian conflict.
